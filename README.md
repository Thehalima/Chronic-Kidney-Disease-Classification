# Chronic-Kidney-Disease-Classification

Chronic kidney disease (CKD) is a progressive condition charcterised by the gradual loss of renal function over time (NHS, 2023). Approximately 7.2 million people are affected by CKD in the UK, and in 2023 it was ranked the ninth leading cause of death globally (Mark et al., 2025). Despite its burden, CKD remains frequently undetected in early stages due to its asymptomatic nature. Patients often prresent only after significant or irreversible renal damage has occured
